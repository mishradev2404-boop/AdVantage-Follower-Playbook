Section 01

Followers Are Not a Vanity Metric

Your Company Page follower count is one of the most strategically undervalued assets in your entire LinkedIn advertising ecosystem. Here's why it matters far more than most advertisers realise.

“Most LinkedIn advertisers treat follower count as a status symbol. The smartest ones treat it as a strategic asset that reduces their cost-per-result over time.”

— AdVantage Media

Organic Amplification

Every follower you gain becomes a free distribution node. When you post organic content, it automatically surfaces in their feed — building a compounding, zero-cost reach channel with every new follower you add. The larger your follower base, the more impressions each post earns without any ad spend.

Algorithm Trust Signal

LinkedIn's algorithm heavily weights the first 60–90 minutes of engagement on any post. A larger follower base means more early interactions, signalling quality content to the platform. This triggers broader organic distribution — meaning pages with more followers get a built-in algorithmic advantage on every post they publish.

Sustained Retargeting Pool

Followers represent a warm, first-party audience that has already expressed genuine interest in your brand. LinkedIn lets you retarget Company Page followers directly in Campaign Manager — turning your organic follower growth into a high-intent paid audience you didn't have to pay to build from scratch.

Your follower count appears on every single paid ad you run.

A higher follower count builds immediate social proof directly within the ad unit — increasing click-through rates and follow rates before a user has even read your copy. It's one of the few organic metrics that directly improves your paid ad performance.

Section 02

Carousels and Videos Drive the Most Social Actions

Your ad format is just as strategic as your targeting. Choosing the right creative format is the single fastest way to accelerate follower growth.

Carousel ads and video ads generate the highest volume of social actions — likes, comments, shares, and reposts — of any format on LinkedIn. This isn't a coincidence. Multi-frame and motion-based content demands more cognitive engagement from the viewer, which translates directly into more interactions.

Social actions are the engine of viral reach. Every time a user interacts with your ad, that interaction is visible to their entire first-degree network — organically. You earn additional, highly-qualified impressions without paying for them. This is sometimes called the "social proof flywheel."

Because carousel and video ads build social momentum faster, the algorithm rewards them with broader distribution. As engagement grows, reach grows, followers grow — and each new follower makes your next post more powerful.

Pro Tip

For carousels, lead with a provocative insight or surprising stat on the first card. The first card is the hook — if it doesn't stop the scroll, no one sees the rest.

Choose Engagement — It Unlocks the Follow Button

The objective you select in LinkedIn Campaign Manager does more than just tell the algorithm what to optimise for — it physically changes what your ad looks like.

Every Engagement objective ad displays a Follow button in the top-right corner — turning every impression into a potential follower.

Many advertisers mistakenly choose Brand Awareness or Website Visits when trying to grow their audience. However, there is a distinct structural advantage to selecting the Engagement objective in LinkedIn Campaign Manager.

When you run campaigns under the Engagement objective, LinkedIn automatically alters the ad unit itself. Every single ad is displayed with an extra "+ Follow" button prominently pinned in the top-right corner of the creative header.

Other objectives do not consistently show this button. This one UI feature alone dramatically increases your follower conversion rate because it removes friction entirely. Users don't need to click through to your Company Page, wait for it to load, and then find the follow button. They can follow you instantly, directly from their feed.

If follower growth is your goal, Engagement is the only objective that makes structural sense. Every other objective actively works against you by hiding or removing the follow action from the ad unit.

Section 04

How LinkedIn's Engagement Optimisation Actually Works

Beyond the UI changes, selecting the Engagement objective fundamentally alters how LinkedIn's algorithm bids and delivers your ads in the auction — and understanding this gives you a real edge.

1. Algorithmic Targeting

LinkedIn's algorithm is instructed to find and serve your ads specifically to members who have a historical track record of taking actions—liking, commenting, sharing, or following—rather than passive scrollers.

2. Lower Cost Per Action

Because the algorithm self-selects for highly receptive audiences, your Cost Per Engagement (CPE) tends to be significantly lower than broad CPM buying. Your budget works harder.

3. Compounding Effect

Over time, this builds a flywheel: more initial engagement signals lead to more organic reach, which drives more followers, creating stronger social proof on all your future ad campaigns.

The Engagement Auction: Step by Step

How LinkedIn decides who sees your ad when you run on the Engagement objective

1

You set your bid and audience

You define your targeting parameters and either set a manual bid or use LinkedIn's automated bidding. For follower growth, we recommend starting with automated bidding to let the algorithm find the most cost-efficient engagers.

2

LinkedIn scores each member for engagement propensity

Before serving your ad to anyone, LinkedIn's system scores every potential impression based on that member's historical engagement behaviour — how often they like, comment, share, or follow from ads. High-propensity members get prioritised.

3

The algorithm bids more for high-engagement profiles

The system will automatically bid higher to win impressions from members who are statistically more likely to engage. This means your CPM may vary across audiences, but your Cost Per Engagement (CPE) stays low because you're only paying for receptive audiences.

4

Early engagement triggers organic amplification

Because LinkedIn's algorithm favours content with strong early engagement signals, the interactions generated by your Engagement-objective campaign can trigger broader organic distribution — extending your reach beyond your paid audience at no additional cost.

Section 05

Spotlight Ads: The Hidden Gem of LinkedIn Advertising

Low CPMs. Low CPCs. Massive Reach Potential.

Spotlight Ads (Dynamic Ads) are one of the most forgotten and underutilised ad formats on LinkedIn. Most advertisers overlook them because they live in the right-rail or sidebar rather than in the main news feed — but this positioning is exactly why they are so highly efficient.

Because fewer advertisers compete for Spotlight Ad inventory, CPMs are significantly lower than Sponsored Content in the feed. LinkedIn's right-rail is a less contested auction, which means your budget stretches much further — you get more impressions per pound or dollar spent.

When paired with a broad audience (e.g., all LinkedIn members in a target country, or an entire job function), Spotlight Ads can deliver extremely high reach at a fraction of the cost. For follower growth campaigns specifically, they work as a high-frequency, low-cost touchpoint that keeps your brand continuously visible to potential followers.

Spotlight Ads are also dynamic by nature — LinkedIn automatically pulls in the viewer's profile photo and personalises the copy (e.g., "[First Name], follow AdVantage for expert LinkedIn tips"). This personalisation dramatically increases relevance and click-through, without requiring you to create individual variations.

When to Use Spotlight Ads for Follower Growth

✅ Use them when...

  • You have a limited budget
  • You want maximum reach
  • You're targeting broad audiences
  • You want always-on brand presence

⚠️ Watch out for...

  • Ad fatigue in small audiences
  • Lower engagement vs. feed ads
  • Right-rail only (desktop)
  • Less creative control

Why Spotlight Ads Win:

  • 1

    Spotlight Ads often have 50–70% lower CPMs than main feed placements.

  • 2

    Broad audiences + Spotlight Ads = maximum reach efficiency for brand awareness.

  • 3

    Most advertisers ignore them entirely — giving you a major competitive advantage in the auction.
